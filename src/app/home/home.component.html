What is ECS?

ECS (Entity Component System) is an architecture where state and behaviour are separated into components and systems. Components (state/data) are associated with an entity and systems (behaviour/function) are executed to read and mutate components.

Why ECS?

Components can be mixed and matched together to affect the behaviour of an entity. This composition based design allows for a greater amount of flexibility than other design patterns such as inheritance.

Commit to Ecsact without committing to an engine.

Ecsact is designed to not rely on any particular engine, affording you the flexibility to make the best decision for your product.

Unity integration is available now with Unreal and Godot support planned for the near future. Custom integration is also possible for in-house or other engines that are not officially planned for.

Interrupt your simulation with Actions

Ecsact introduces actions to your ECS. Actions are a specialized system used to break-up the simulation. Perfectly suitable for user or bot input.

Build your systems with your choice of programming language.

Ecsact does not discriminate between programming languages. The choice is yours. Systems may be implemented in any language that can be exported to WebAssembly.

System implemenations may also be in different formats or even set dynamically at runtime!
